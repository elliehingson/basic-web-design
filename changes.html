Monarch Butterfly Metamorphosis

Egg

The life of a monarch begins when a female lays tiny, cream-colored eggs—usually on the underside of milkweed leaves, the only plant monarch caterpillars eat. Eggs hatch in about 3 to 5 days.

Larva (Caterpillar)

Once hatched, the caterpillar begins eating milkweed almost immediately. Over the course of about 10–14 days, it grows rapidly, molting (shedding its skin) five times. During this stage, it develops the distinct black, white, and yellow stripes characteristic of monarch larvae.

Pupa (Chrysalis)

After reaching full size, the caterpillar attaches itself to a surface and forms a jade-green chrysalis with gold accents. Inside this protective casing, the caterpillar's body undergoes an extraordinary transformation. This stage lasts about 10 days.

Adult Butterfly

A fully formed monarch butterfly emerges from the chrysalis. After its wings dry and harden, it flies off in search of nectar and mates. Depending on the season, some adults migrate thousands of miles to overwintering sites in Mexico or California.
